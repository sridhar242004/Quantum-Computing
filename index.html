Foundations

Core Quantum Concepts

Build your intuition for quantum mechanics through carefully crafted explanations of the fundamental principles that power quantum computing.

🌊
Superposition

Unlike classical bits, qubits can exist in multiple states simultaneously — a fundamental property enabling quantum parallelism and exponential computational power over classical systems.

Ask AI about this →
🔗
Entanglement

Two or more qubits become quantum mechanically linked, so measuring one instantly determines the state of the other — regardless of the distance separating them.

Ask AI about this →
🌀
Interference

Quantum algorithms harness wave-like interference to amplify correct answers and cancel incorrect ones — analogous to how noise-cancelling headphones work, but for computation.

Ask AI about this →
📐
Quantum Gates

The quantum equivalents of classical logic gates — unitary transformations on qubits including the Hadamard gate, CNOT, Toffoli, and the Pauli X, Y, Z matrices.

Ask AI about this →
👁️
Measurement

Measuring a qubit collapses its superposition to a definite classical state — a probabilistic process governed by Born's rule where probability equals the amplitude squared.

Ask AI about this →
🛡️
Error Correction

Quantum error correction encodes logical qubits across multiple physical qubits, protecting against decoherence and gate errors — the key to fault-tolerant quantum computation.

Ask AI about this →
